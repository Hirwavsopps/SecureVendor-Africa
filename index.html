[01]

Use a Password Manager

Never reuse passwords across accounts. Tools like Bitwarden (free) or 1Password generate and store unique strong passwords for every site you use.

[02]

Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Add a second verification step on all accounts — especially email, banking, and mobile money. Even if your password is stolen, 2FA blocks access.

[03]

Verify Before You Click

Hover over links before clicking. Check sender email addresses carefully. Legitimate banks and services will never ask for passwords via email or SMS.

[04]

Keep Software Updated

Most cyberattacks exploit known vulnerabilities in outdated software. Enable automatic updates on your OS, browser, and apps to stay protected.

[05]

Backup Your Data — The 3-2-1 Rule

Keep 3 copies of important data, on 2 different storage types, with 1 copy offsite or in the cloud. This protects against ransomware and hardware failure.

[06]

Use HTTPS Websites Only

Always check for the padlock icon and "https://" before entering any sensitive information on a website. Avoid submitting forms on HTTP sites.
